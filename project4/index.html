What is Mars?

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. The surface of Mars is orange-red because it is
covered in iron(III) oxide dust, giving it the nickname "the Red Planet".

Why is Mars so special?

Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are believed to be
captured asteroids. These moons are much smaller and irregularly shaped compared to Earth's moon.

Mars has a very thin atmosphere compared to Earth. This thin atmosphere
contributes to its extreme temperature variations and lack of liquid water on the surface..

Due to Mars' greater distance from the Sun compared to Earth,
the Sun would appear about half the size as seen from Earth.
This translates to a less intense sunlight experience on the Martian surface..
